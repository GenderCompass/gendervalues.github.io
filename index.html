Gender Compass

What is Gender Compass?

Gender Compass is a quiz for people questioning their gender inspired by and based upon the 8values quiz that helps you understand your gender and point you in a direction that might help you understand yourself better. You will be provided with an honest answer based on how you feel with each answer slightly affecting your scores. The categories for each question will be random to avoid any bias you might have to the results you want. This is just for fun it's by no means a diagnosis with gender dysphoria or a be all and end all for your identity. This quiz just provides you with a stepping stone for an identity you may want to explore. I encourage you to do more research about your results and ask questions and experiment. It's okay if it's not the identity for you. :)

What are the identities?

There are currently six identities, each of which has two opposing values. They are:

Masculine vs. Non-Masculine
This is based primarily off of identity, comfort with masculine perception and discomfort with being treated as something else. Not stereotypes. The more masculine identify with the closer to the male identity you get and vice versa.

Feminine vs. Non-Feminine
This is also based primarily off of identity, comfort with feminine perception and discomfort with being treated as something else. Not stereotypes. The more feminine identify with the closer to the female identity you get and vice versa.

Aporagender vs. Non-aporagender
Aporagender is an umbrella term for genders outside the spectrum of masculine and feminine and also outside genderlessness while still feeling like a gender. This might be seen as a third gender in some cultures. The the higher your aporagender score the more likely you are to be outside the gender binary and identify as something else completly. The lower your aporagender score the more likely you are to identify with binary identities or no gender at all.

Multigender vs. Agender
Multigender is when you identify as more than one gender and agender is when you identify as none. This is a sliding scale between feeling like many genders and feeling like none with a score closer to the middle meaning you identify closer with 1.

Genderfluidity vs. Genderstill
Genderfluidity is when your gender changes over the course of time having a higher genderfluid score means that your gender changes often. Being genderstill is when that your gender stays the same and doesn't change. Having a high genderstill score means that your gender is static. A score closer to the middle means you're Genderflux which is when your gender stays the same but changes in intensity.

What does "Closest Match" mean in the results?

This quiz will try to match you with a gender identity based on your answers to the quiz and where you fall on all the axes. These genders are Man, Woman, Agender, Ambonec, Androgyne, Aporagender, Bigender, Mascflux, Femmeflux, Demigirl, Demiboy, Genderfluid, Genderqueer, Maverique and Multigender.

I don't like my scores!

That's totally okay. A quiz can't determine who you are as a person. This is just meant to be a step in the right direction to figure out who you are. Experimenting with labels and coming up with your own is perfectly valid. If you have any ways I can improve this quix you can open an issue on the GitHub Page.
